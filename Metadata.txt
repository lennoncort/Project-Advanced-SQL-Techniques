# Metadata Summary

This repository includes three datasets related to the City of Chicago.  
The following summary provides user-friendly descriptions of each dataset, their purpose, scope, and key details.  

---

## 1. Crimes - 2001 to Present  
**Domain:** Public Safety  
- Contains reported incidents of crime in Chicago from 2001 to the present (excluding the most recent 7 days).  
- Data comes from the Chicago Police Department’s CLEAR (Citizen Law Enforcement Analysis and Reporting) system.  
- **Privacy protection:** Addresses are provided at the block level only; specific locations are not disclosed.  
- **Caveats:**  
  - Records are based on preliminary reports and may be reclassified after investigation.  
  - Potential for human/mechanical error.  
  - Not intended for precise time-comparison studies.  
- **Updates:** Daily.  
- **More info:** Chicago Police Department - Illinois Uniform Crime Reporting (IUCR) codes: http://data.cityofchicago.org/Public-Safety/Chicago-Police-Department-Illinois-Uniform-Crime-R/c7ck-438e  

---

## 2. Chicago Public Schools - Progress Report Cards (2011–2012)  
**Domain:** Education  
- Provides performance metrics for all schools in the Chicago Public Schools system during the 2011–2012 academic year.  
- **Indicators include:**  
  - Survey-based metrics: Student Safety, Family Involvement, Supportive Environment, Ambitious Instruction, Effective Leadership, Collaborative Teachers, Parent Engagement.  
  - Attendance rates: Students and teachers.  
  - Academic performance: Literacy & Math benchmarks (PK–8), ISAT test scores, growth metrics, algebra enrollment/passing, and ACT/PLAN/EXPLORE assessments.  
  - College readiness & outcomes: Graduation rate, college eligibility, college enrollment.  
- **Special codes:** “NDA” = No Data Available.  
- **Purpose:** Tracks school performance, environment, and readiness for higher education.  

---

## 3. Socioeconomic Indicators and Hardship Index (2008–2012)  
**Domain:** Public Health & Community Development  
- Covers socioeconomic conditions across Chicago’s community areas.  
- **Six core indicators:**  
  1. % of occupied housing units with >1 person per room (crowded housing).  
  2. % of households below the federal poverty level.  
  3. % of unemployed persons (16+ years in the labor force).  
  4. % of population (25+ years) without a high school diploma.  
  5. % of population under 18 or over 64 (dependency ratio).  
  6. Per capita income.  
- Includes a **Hardship Index** summarizing the above indicators.  
- Final row includes aggregate indicators for the City of Chicago as a whole.  
- **Reference:** Full dataset description: https://data.cityofchicago.org/api/views/fwb8-6aw5/files/A5KBlegGR2nWI1jgP6pjJl32CTPwPbkl9KU3FxlZk-A?download=true  

---

## Notes for Users  
- These datasets complement each other, allowing cross-domain analysis of crime, education, and socioeconomic conditions in Chicago.  
- While highly valuable, users should keep in mind reporting limitations, missing data, and the contextual nature of survey-based and administrative indicators.  
- Data are provided for research, analysis, and educational purposes.
